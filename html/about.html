Why do I want to become a web developer?

    Continuous learning

    One of the main reasons why I would like to change my career and become a software developer is that I have reached a learning ceiling in my current job and find it difficult to progress and develop further. Something I love about software development is that you never stop learning and there is always room to grow and improve.

    Creative work

    Before I started coding, I never thought of software development as a tool I could use to express my creativity. Learning to code completely changed my view on that. I love how coding allows you to use your creativity to build and create your own designs as well as to find solutions to problems.

    Problem solving

    I have always loved solving puzzles and finding solutions to problems. There is nothing like the feeling of using your brain to find an answer. I love the satisfaction you get when debugging and making something work. I am aware that sometimes you may not be able to find a solution to a problem for days, but nothing beats the feeling of finally solving it.

    Power of tech

    Since I left university, I have always wanted to work for a company that is a force for good. That's why, in my current and previous jobs, I worked for small companies with strong sustainable values. In recent years, many of the new companies I admire have one thing in common: they use tech as a tool to change the world. Some good examples are Olio, Beam, TooGoodToGo, or The Good Gym. I feel extremely excited to have the necessary skills to write the code for some tech that will change the world for the better.

    I really enjoy coding

    Since day one, I have found coding fascinating. I could spend hours coding and not notice the time pass. Like with everything, there have been harder times when I would get stuck on a problem and struggle to find a solution, but managing to solve the challenges made it so worth it.

What have been my progress like?

In Januray, when I decided to give the application a go I didn't know anything about coding. I started reading the resources and followed every step in A complete guide to making web pages from scratch by Oliver Jam and MDN: Getting started with the web. Without noticing, I found myself learning to code for hours without realising time passing by.

At the start there was a steep learning curve as every concept was new. I would read an explanation and would have to google several words in a sentence to try to understand the simplest tutorial:

What does deploy mean?

What is the terminal?

How do I find it?

What is repostory?

Fastfoward three months and after studying an average of 5.5 days a week, I now know the difference between Github and Git, I can easily use buttons to make a photo appear or disappear, I can tell people where I've deployed my websit, how I iterate throgh a loop and more importantly - I'm not scared of JavaScript anymore.

Why Founders and Coders?

    I love how FAC works to make the industry more accessible

    It's difficult to explain, but even though I found out about Founders & Coders in November, I thought it was not for me. It felt like it was too out of my reach, and I didn't allow myself to even start reading about the first project. I am the first generation in my family to go to university as my parents only finished primary school. I don't have any references to people close to me who are in the industry, and being a woman made it all seem extra intimidating.
    Then, in January, I received an email from Sarah inviting me to the Support Circle. It made me think that maybe it was for me as well. I decided to start looking at the resources, and once I started learning about how to create my Hobby Page, it turned out that I loved it and couldn't stop learning.
    I feel extremely grateful to Founders and Coders for having shown me what a great collaborative environment this industry is. By going to the meet-ups, I was able to meet other people who were on the same path, and we were all helping and supporting each other.

    FAC is not an intimidating space

    Founders and Coders creates a great collaborative environment where everyone supports each other. This, together with FAC's promise to diversify the industry, makes me feel encouraged to learn coding and not intimidated by the learning environment.

    I am an absolute believer in Peer Learning

    In my academic life, I have always learned through teacher-centered experiences. Founders and Coders has given me the opportunity to experience a peer learning approach, and I loved it. It created a great team spirit where we could relate to the difficulties we were having and where we would all be encouraging each other. It was also surprising how much you gained from helping out others - it was a very rewarding experience that helped reinforce your learning.

    Positive impact

    One of the things that caught my attention when I found Founders and Coders was their program Tech for Better. Ethics and values are very important to me, and seeing that program reassured me that FAC's values aligned with mine, and it would be a good place for me to learn.
